Material Testing

  • Cube Test
    Measures the compressive strength of concrete by testing cube-shaped samples, ensuring the material meets design specifications.
  • Brick Testing
    Evaluates the quality, strength, and water absorption capacity of bricks to ensure their suitability for construction.
  • Cement Testing
    Analyzes properties like setting time, fineness, and strength of cement to ensure compliance with industry standards.
  • Concrete Testing
    Determines the workability, strength, and durability of concrete mixtures used in construction projects.
  • Steel Testing
    Assesses the tensile strength, ductility, and elasticity of steel to verify its performance under various loads.
  • Bitumen Testing
    Examines the quality and properties of bitumen, such as viscosity and penetration, to ensure reliable road construction.

Structural Testing

  • Load Testing
    Checks the structural integrity by applying loads to assess its ability to bear weight under designed conditions.
  • Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)
    Inspects materials and structures for defects without causing damage, ensuring safety and reliability.
  • Deflection Testing
    Measures the displacement or bending of a structure under load to evaluate its flexibility and strength.
  • Flexural Testing
    Tests the bending strength of materials like beams to ensure they can withstand expected stresses.

Geotechnical Testing

  • Standard Penetration Test (SPT)
    Determines soil density and strength by measuring resistance to a standardized penetration.
  • Cone Penetration Test (CPT)
    Evaluates soil properties by driving a cone into the ground and measuring resistance and friction.
  • Pile Load Test
    Assesses the load-bearing capacity of foundation piles under static conditions.
  • Pile Integrity Test (PIT)
    Detects structural soundness and potential flaws in foundation piles using wave propagation techniques.
  • Pile Dynamic Analysis (PDA)
    Measures the dynamic response of piles to evaluate load capacity and soil resistance.
  • Friction Testing
    Determines the frictional resistance of bored piles for accurate foundation design.
  • Radial Load Test
    Tests lateral load-bearing capacity, often used in soil stabilization and foundation analysis.

Land Survey

  • Road Survey
    Maps and assesses road alignment and gradient for efficient and safe construction of roadways.
  • Building Survey
    Measures land and building dimensions to ensure proper construction alignment and compliance with designs.
  • Leveling Survey
    Determines elevation differences across land to ensure proper leveling for construction projects.
  • Real Estate Projects
    Surveys land for real estate development, including zoning, marking, and mapping for projects.
  • Topographical Surveys
    Captures land features such as elevations, contours, and natural elements for project planning.
  • Layout Design & Marking
    Designs and marks layouts on-site to align with architectural plans and ensure accuracy in execution.
